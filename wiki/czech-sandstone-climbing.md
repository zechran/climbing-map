# Czech Sandstone Climbing

**Summary**: The ethics, style, and gear conventions of traditional climbing on Czech sandstone towers, a distinct discipline with strict rules about fixed protection.

**Sources**: piskari.cz, ovkbroumovsko.cz

**Last updated**: 2026-05-25

---

Czech sandstone climbing (pískovcové lezení, or "lezení na písku" — climbing on sand) is a traditional discipline practised on the soft sandstone towers of the [[policka-panev]] and other Czech sandstone regions (e.g. Labské pískovce, Český ráj). It has its own strict ethical code that differs significantly from sport or alpine climbing.

## Core ethics

The fundamental rule is **no permanent metal protection** drilled into the rock. The sandstone is soft and easily damaged; drilling bolts is considered both physically destructive and ethically unacceptable. All protection must be **removable and placed by the leader on the ascent**.

## Gear types

Czech sandstone climbers use a specific vocabulary for protection:

- **Smyčka** (pl. smyčky) — sling/loop; threaded through natural constrictions in cracks or behind flakes
- **Bambule** — a threaded runner using a knotted rope or cord jammed into a crack; equivalent to a hex or thread. Can be "pofidérní" (dubious/suspect) or solid. A "malá bambule" is a small one.
- **Uzel** (pl. uzly) — knot; a simple knotted cord placed in a crack
- **Kruh** (pl. kruhy) — ring; a fixed metal ring (the only allowed fixed protection), usually placed on first ascent by the FA team and used as a clip point
- **Erárka** — a government/state-era fixed ring or old fixed protection; legacy gear from older ascents
- **Matrosh** — the soft, crumbly surface layer of sandstone; can be unreliable for placements
- **Sokolík** — a specific body technique / narrow chimney size where you can press out between two walls using knees and back; literal meaning is "little falcon"
- **Rajbák** — a wider chimney technique using pressure between walls (wider than sokolík)
- **Žaba** — "frog" — an even wider chimney/flare technique
- **Širočina** — very wide crack / offwidth

## Fixed protection: rings (kruhy)

The only permanent protection allowed is the **kruh** (ring), placed during the first ascent and bolted into the rock by the FA party. Rings serve as clip points (similar to bolts in sport climbing) but are placed sparsely, only where absolutely necessary. Climbers refer to "1.Q", "2.Q", "3.Q" etc. to indicate the first, second, third ring on a route.

## First ascent ethics (prvovýstupy)

First ascent rules are governed by [[ovk-broumovsko]]. A current debate concerns **saské stavění** (Saxon-style placing) — a method of placing gear from above (top-down) before the first ascent, which some consider contrary to ground-up tradition. This is an active governance discussion as of 2026.

## Seasonal closures

Climbing may be restricted during bird nesting season (especially peregrine falcons — sokol stěhovavý). Closures are announced by [[ovk-broumovsko]].
