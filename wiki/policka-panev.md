# Polická pánev (Police Basin)

**Summary**: Geographic region in northeastern Czechia containing five major Czech sandstone climbing areas, all governed by OVK Broumovsko.

**Sources**: piskari.cz, ovkbroumovsko.cz

**Last updated**: 2026-05-25

---

The Polická pánev (Police Basin) is the collective name for the sandstone rock landscape in the Broumov region of northeastern Czechia (Královéhradecký kraj), near the Polish border. It is the heartland of [[czech-sandstone-climbing]] and home to five distinct climbing areas.

## Climbing areas

- [[adrspach]] — Adršpašské skály; the largest and most visited area, with multiple named sectors
- [[teplicke-skaly]] — Teplické skály; adjacent to Adršpach, forms the broader Adršpašsko-teplické skály nature reserve
- [[ostas]] — Ostaš; divided into Horní Ostaš and Zadní Ostaš sub-areas
- [[broumovske-steny]] — Broumovské stěny; sandstone cliff band
- [[krizovy-vrch]] — Křižový vrch (also called Křížák)

## Administration & access

All five areas fall under the jurisdiction of [[ovk-broumovsko]]. Access rules, first ascent ethics, seasonal nesting closures, and route maintenance are all coordinated through this body.

The area lies within a protected natural landscape (CHKO Broumovsko), which means climbing is subject to nature conservation agreements. Some towers or sections may be subject to seasonal closures (e.g. peregrine falcon nesting).

## Geographic neighbours

The Polish side of the border (Góry Stołowe / Stolové hory) is a related sandstone landscape. [[piskari-cz]] has cross-border promotional links with this area via EU funding.
