Adršpach (Ádr)

Summary: The largest and most visited Czech sandstone climbing area in the Polická pánev. Divided into sub-areas (Dolní Adršpach, Horní Adršpach, etc.), each containing named sectors, which in turn contain individual towers with routes.

Sources: piskari.cz, ovkbroumovsko.cz

Last updated: 2026-05-25

Adršpach (colloquially “Ádr”) is the premier sandstone climbing destination in the Polická pánev (Police Basin). The rock formations form a dramatic “rock town” (skalní město) of free-standing towers, gorges, and corridors. Together with Teplické skály (Teplice) it forms the broader Adršpašsko-teplické skály national nature reserve.

Hierarchy

The wiki uses the following hierarchy within this area:

Area (Adršpach) → Sub-area → Sector → Tower → Route → Comments

Sub-areas group sectors geographically. Sectors group towers. Each tower has GPS coordinates (from piskari.cz JS map) and route data (from piskari.cz).

Season

The Adršpach climbing season traditionally opens in June (mid-summer). An article on piskari.cz noted “two months to the start of the Adršpach season” in April 2026, suggesting an early-to-mid June opening. This may be due to spring moisture in the sandstone.
